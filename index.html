Some important information about plants

1. Chafa Red

  • Botanical Name: Plumeria rubra
  • Category: Shrub/Small Tree
  • Characteristics:
    • Deciduous plant with thick, fleshy branches
    • Red, fragrant flowers
    • Grows up to 8 meters tall
    • Flowers bloom mainly in summer
    • Prefers full sun and well-drained soil
  • Medicinal Values:
    • Used in traditional medicine to treat inflammation
    • Bark and leaves used for healing wounds
    • Flowers used in skin care preparations
    • Decoction of bark used as a purgative
    • Extracts used to relieve cough and fever
    • Can act as a mild laxative
    • Antibacterial properties

2. Chafa Pink

  • Botanical Name: Plumeria rubra (Pink variety)
  • Category: Shrub/Small Tree
  • Characteristics:
    • Similar to red chafa but with pink flowers
    • Strong fragrance, especially in the evening
    • Thick succulent branches and broad leaves
    • Grows best in tropical/subtropical climates
    • Often used as an ornamental plant
  • Medicinal Values:
    • Used to relieve itching and skin irritations
    • Mild analgesic properties
    • Helps treat ulcers and sores
    • Decoction used to reduce fever
    • Antifungal applications
    • Used in Ayurveda for detoxifying the body
    • May assist in digestive health

3. Mango

  • Botanical Name: Mangifera indica
  • Category: Tree
  • Characteristics:
    • Evergreen, tall tree up to 30 meters
    • Large, leathery leaves
    • Produces sweet, fleshy fruit
    • Flowers are small, white and fragrant
    • Common in tropical regions
  • Medicinal Values:
    • Mango leaves help manage diabetes
    • Fruit is rich in vitamins A, C, and E
    • Bark used for diarrhea and dysentery
    • Seed powder used for dental health
    • Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties
    • Promotes digestion and gut health
    • Raw mango used to treat heatstroke
    • Boosts immunity

4. Chameli (Jasmine)

 

  • Botanical Name: Jasminum grandiflorum or Jasminum officinale
  • Category: Climbing Shrub
  • Characteristics:
    • White, star-shaped, intensely fragrant flowers
    • Glossy green leaves
    • Blooms mostly in the evening/night
    • Common in warm, sunny areas
    • Used widely in perfumes and oils
  • Medicinal Values:
    • Oil helps relieve stress and anxiety
    • Used to treat skin problems and infections
    • Antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties
    • Flowers can aid in boosting mood
    • Used in treating headaches and insomnia
    • Helps regulate hormones
    • Tea made from flowers aids digestion

 

5. Mogra (Arabian Jasmine)

 

  • Botanical Name: Jasminum sambac
  • Category: Shrub
  • Characteristics:
    • Small white flowers, very fragrant
    • Blooms throughout the year
    • Compact growth, ideal for gardens and pots
    • Used in making garlands and perfumes
    • Loves warm, humid climates
  • Medicinal Values:
    • Helps relieve stress and promote sleep
    • Used in aromatherapy
    • Treats wounds and skin conditions
    • Infusion used to reduce fever
    • Antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects
    • Helps in menstrual discomfort
    • Mogra tea promotes relaxation and gut health
